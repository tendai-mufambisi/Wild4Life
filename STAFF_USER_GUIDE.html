1. What is the Dashboard?

Your command centre for the entire Wild4Life website

The Wild4Life Dashboard is a private, staff-only area of the Wild4Life website. Think of it as the "back office" — while visitors see the public website at the front, you use the dashboard behind the scenes to manage everything on it.


From the dashboard you can:


📈 See Donations

View every donation made in real time, see who gave, how much, and in which currency.

✍️ Publish Blog Posts

Write news, updates, and stories directly from your browser — no coding needed.

👥 Manage the Team

Add, update, or remove board members and staff that appear on the public website.

📷 Manage the Gallery

Upload new photos and organise the gallery that the public sees.

✭ Manage Testimonials

Add quotes and feedback from community members, partners, and stakeholders.

⚙️ Configure the Site

Update contact details, social media links, and registration certificates.

💡

You do not need to know how to code. Every action in the dashboard is point-and-click. If you can use Facebook or WhatsApp, you can use this dashboard.

2. Installing the App on Your Phone

Add Wild4Life to your home screen like a regular app — no app store needed

Wild4Life is a Progressive Web App (PWA). This means you can install it on your phone or computer so it opens instantly like a normal app — without going through the Google Play Store or Apple App Store. It also works offline for certain features.

Installing on Android (Chrome)

  • Open the Wild4Life website in Chrome

    Type the website address into Chrome and open the public homepage.

  • Wait for the install banner to appear

    After about 5 seconds, a small banner at the bottom of the screen will say "Add Wild4Life to your home screen". Tap it.

  • Tap "Install" or "Add"

    A small pop-up will confirm. Tap the button and the Wild4Life icon will appear on your home screen.

  • Open the app from your home screen

    It opens in full-screen mode, just like a regular app — no browser bars visible.

💡

If the banner does not appear automatically, tap the three dots (⋮) menu in Chrome's top-right corner, then select "Add to Home Screen" or "Install App".

Installing on iPhone (Safari)

  • Open Safari

    The install feature only works in Safari on iPhone — not Chrome or Firefox.

  • Go to the Wild4Life website

    Visit the public homepage in Safari.

  • Tap the Share button

    This is the square with an upward arrow, found at the bottom of the screen in Safari.

  • Scroll down and tap "Add to Home Screen"

    Give it a name (or leave as "Wild4Life") and tap Add in the top-right corner.

  • Open the app from your home screen

    The Wild4Life icon will appear on your iPhone home screen like any other app.

Installing on a Computer (Windows or Mac)

  • Open Chrome or Edge on your computer

    Go to the Wild4Life website.

  • Look for the install icon in the address bar

    A small computer screen icon (⊕) or download arrow will appear at the right end of the address bar.

  • Click it and confirm

    Click "Install". Wild4Life will open in its own window and a shortcut will appear on your desktop.

3. How to Access the Dashboard

Finding the hidden staff login — the secret button

The dashboard login is not advertised on the public website — this keeps it safe from unauthorized visitors. It is accessed through a hidden button tucked away at the very bottom of every page on the website.

Finding the Secret Button

  • Go to the Wild4Life public website

    Open any page — the home page, about page, or any other public page.

  • Scroll all the way to the very bottom

    Go past all the content, the footer links, the contact info — to the very last line of the page.

  • Look for the tiny black square in the credits line

    At the very bottom, you will see a line that reads "Powered by Palset Digital". Right next to it there is a tiny black square (■) — that is the secret staff portal button.

  • Click or tap the black square

    This will take you directly to the staff login page.

■
This tiny square — at the very bottom of every page — is your door to the dashboard
⚠️

Keep this secret. Do not share the location of this button or the dashboard address with members of the public. Only share it with authorised staff members.

💡

Shortcut for regular use: Once you know the dashboard URL, you can bookmark it directly or use it as your starting point. The dashboard also appears as a shortcut in the installed PWA app.

4. Logging In

Signing in to your staff account

Once you have reached the login page, signing in is straightforward.


  • Enter your Username

    This was given to you when your account was created. It is usually your first name or initials.

  • Enter your Password

    Type your password carefully. Passwords are case-sensitive (uppercase and lowercase letters matter).

  • Click "Sign In"

    If your details are correct, you will be taken directly to the Analytics overview page.

🚫

Cannot log in? Only accounts that have been set up as staff accounts can log in here. If you see an error, contact your administrator to check that your account exists and is active. You cannot log in with a regular donor or public-facing account.

💡

Forgot your password? Ask your system administrator to reset it for you through the dashboard's Team & Roles section. See Section 13 for details.

5. Understanding Your Role & Permissions

What you can see and do depends on your assigned role

Every staff member is assigned one of three roles. Your role determines which sections of the dashboard you can access. This is by design — it keeps sensitive information visible only to those who need it.

Role What They Can Do Typical Person
Admin Full access to everything: analytics, donations, donors, all blog posts, staff accounts, team members, testimonials, gallery, FAQs, and site settings. Executive Director, IT Manager
Manager Analytics, donations & donors, all blog posts (can publish), team, testimonials, gallery, and FAQs. Cannot manage staff accounts or site settings. Programme Manager, Communications Officer
Writer Can only write blog posts — but only their own drafts. Cannot publish posts (a manager or admin must approve and publish). Cannot see donations or donors. Content Writer, Volunteer
💡

If you need access to a section that is not appearing for you, ask an Admin to upgrade your role. Your role is shown by name on the Team & Roles page.

6. The Analytics Overview

Your live window into donations, donors, and financial performance

The first page you see after logging in is the Analytics Overview (also called the Dashboard Home). It shows you a complete live picture of how donations are performing — no spreadsheet needed.

The Stat Cards

Across the top of the page you will see ten coloured information boxes — called stat cards. Each one shows a key number at a glance:

💲
Total Raised (USD)
The total amount of all confirmed donations received in US Dollars.
💳
Total Raised (ZWG)
Total confirmed donations received in Zimbabwe Gold (local currency).
👥
Total Donors
The number of unique people who have donated at least once.
✅
Success Rate
The percentage of payment attempts that were completed successfully.
📅
Today's Collections
Donations received today — total amount and number of transactions.
📄
This Month
All donations received during the current calendar month.
🧮
Average Donation
The average amount given per successful donation.
⏳
Pending
Payments that were started but not yet confirmed or failed.
❌
Failed
Payments that were attempted but did not go through.
🚫
Cancelled
Payments that the donor started but then cancelled themselves.

The Charts

Below the stat cards, there are three interactive charts:

📊 Chart 1 — 30-Day Trend Line

Shows the last 30 days of donation activity as a line graph. You can click the buttons above it to switch between viewing the number of donations per day, or the amount collected per day. This helps you spot busy days and quiet periods.

🧸 Chart 2 — Status Breakdown (Donut)

A circular chart showing the all-time split of your donation statuses: how many were paid, pending, failed, or cancelled. The legend on the side shows the exact percentages. A high "Paid" slice and a low "Failed" slice is what you want to see.

🔥 Chart 3 — Monthly Bar Chart

A bar chart showing confirmed USD donations month by month over the past year or more. Great for comparing months and spotting seasonal patterns or campaign results.

Recent Activity Tables

At the bottom of the analytics page, there are two tables:

📋 Recent Donations

Shows the last 10 donation transactions — reference number, donor name, amount, status, and date.

🏆 Top Donors

Shows your most generous supporters — ranked by total amount donated, with their phone and donation count.

💡

The analytics page refreshes every time you open it — you are always seeing live, up-to-date numbers.

7. Donations & Donors

Viewing payment history and supporter information (Manager and Admin only)

🔒

Access required: This section is only visible to Manager and Admin users. Writers do not have access to financial data.

7.1 Viewing Transactions (Donations List)

In the sidebar, click Donations → Transactions to see the full list of all payment records.

Each row in the list shows:

  • Reference: A unique code like W4L-2026-000123 — use this for receipts or queries.
  • Donor Name & Phone: Who made the donation.
  • Amount & Currency: How much and in which currency (USD or ZWG).
  • Status: Paid ✓, Pending ⌛, Failed ✗, or Cancelled ☒.
  • Date & Time: When the payment was made.

You can filter the list by status (e.g. show only "Paid") and by date range to narrow down what you are looking for.

7.2 Viewing the Donor List

In the sidebar, click Donations → Donors to see the list of all unique supporters.

The donor list is sorted by total donated, so your most generous supporters appear first. For each donor you can see:

  • Their full name
  • Phone number and email address
  • Total amount donated (all time)
  • Number of times they have donated

Use the search box at the top to look for a specific donor by name or phone number.

⚠️

Handle donor data with care. Donor names, phone numbers, and email addresses are personal information. Only share this data when absolutely necessary, and never post it publicly.

8. Writing & Publishing Blog Posts

Share news, stories, and updates with the public

Creating a New Blog Post

  • Click "Blog Posts" in the sidebar

    Then click the + New Post button (or "New Post" in the sidebar shortcut).

  • Enter the Post Title

    Write a clear, descriptive title. This is the headline that will appear on the blog page.

  • Write your content in the editor

    The editor works like a simple word processor — you can make text bold, italic, add bullet lists, headings, and more using the toolbar buttons at the top of the writing area.

  • Add a Thumbnail Image (optional but recommended)

    Click the image upload area to add a photo that will appear as the cover image for the blog post in the blog listing.

  • Write an Excerpt (short summary)

    This is a 1–2 sentence summary of the post. It appears in the blog listing to give readers a preview of what the article is about.

  • Set the Status

    Choose Draft to save but not publish yet, or Published to make it live on the website immediately.

  • Click "Save Post"

    Your post is saved. If you set it to Published, it is now live on the Wild4Life website.

💡

Writers note: If your role is Writer, your posts will always be saved as Draft even if you select Published. A Manager or Admin must open your post and change the status to Published to make it go live.

Editing an Existing Post

From the Blog Posts list, find the post you want to change and click the Edit (pencil) button on its row. Make your changes and click Save Post.

Deleting a Post

From the Blog Posts list, click the Delete (bin) button next to the post. You will be asked to confirm before it is permanently removed.

⚠️

Deletion is permanent. Once a blog post is deleted, it cannot be recovered. If you just want to hide a post from the public temporarily, set its status to Draft instead of deleting it.

9. Managing the Team Page

Adding and updating board members and staff that appear on the public website

The public Team page on the Wild4Life website shows two groups: Board Members and Staff Members. You manage both from the dashboard under Site Content → Team.

Adding a New Team Member

  • Go to Site Content → Team in the sidebar

    Then click the Add Member button.

  • Fill in their Name and Role

    For example: Name: Dr. Patience Moyo and Role: Board Chairperson.

  • Select the Member Type

    Choose Board if they sit on the board of directors, or Staff for employees or operational team members.

  • Upload their Photo

    Click the photo field and select an image from your device. Use a clear, professional headshot.

  • Write their Bio

    A short paragraph introducing the person — their background, experience, and what they contribute to Wild4Life.

  • Fill in optional fields

    You may also enter their Years of Experience, Qualifications (one qualification per line), WhatsApp number, and Phone number.

  • Set the Order number

    A lower number means they appear earlier on the page. For example: the Chairperson should have Order 1, the Vice Chair Order 2, and so on.

  • Make sure "Active" is ticked, then save

    Only active members appear on the public website.

💡

To temporarily hide a team member without deleting them, edit their record and un-tick the "Active" box. They will disappear from the public site but remain in your dashboard for future use.

10. Managing Testimonials

Sharing positive feedback and stories from the community

Testimonials appear on the public Testimonials page and help build trust with visitors and potential donors. Go to Site Content → Testimonials to manage them.

Adding a Testimonial

  • Click "Add Testimonial"

    From the Testimonials list page.

  • Enter the person's Name

    For example: Mrs. Chiedza Manyika.

  • Enter their Title or Role

    For example: VHW Supervisor, Binga District.

  • Enter their Location

    For example: Binga, Zimbabwe.

  • Write their Quote

    Type out what they said, in their own words where possible. Keep it genuine and concise.

  • Set the Rating (1–5 stars)

    Choose a rating from 1 to 5. Most testimonials are 4 or 5 stars.

  • Set the Order number and save

    Lower numbers appear first. Make sure "Active" is ticked.

11. Managing the Photo Gallery

Uploading and organising photos that appear on the public gallery page

The Gallery on the public website shows photos of Wild4Life's activities in the field. Go to Site Content → Gallery to upload and manage images.

Uploading Photos

  • Click inside the upload area

    You will see a large drag-and-drop zone on the gallery management page. Click it to open your device's file picker.

  • Select one or more photos

    You can upload multiple photos at the same time by selecting several files at once (hold Ctrl on Windows or Cmd on Mac while clicking).

  • Add a caption (optional)

    Type a brief description of the photo — for example: "Community health dialogue, Lupane 2025". Captions help the public understand what they are looking at.

  • Click "Upload"

    The photos will be saved and appear in the gallery list below.

Organising Gallery Photos

Each uploaded photo has an Order number. Lower numbers appear first on the public gallery. Edit a photo to change its order number and rearrange how photos are displayed.


To remove a photo, click the red Delete button next to it. It will be removed from the gallery immediately. This cannot be undone.

💡

Photo quality tip: Use photos that are at least 800 pixels wide and show your work clearly. Avoid blurry, dark, or very small images. The first 8 photos also appear on the home page carousel.

12. Managing FAQs

Keeping the Frequently Asked Questions on the homepage up to date

FAQs (Frequently Asked Questions) appear on the public home page as an expandable list. They help visitors quickly find answers to common questions. Go to Site Content → FAQs to manage them.

Adding a New FAQ

  • Click "Add FAQ"

    From the FAQs list page.

  • Type the Question

    Write it as a question a member of the public would ask — for example: "How do I donate using mobile money?"

  • Type the Answer

    Give a clear, helpful answer. Keep it simple and to the point.

  • Set the Order number and save

    FAQ with Order 1 appears at the top and is auto-expanded when the page loads. Make sure "Active" is ticked for it to be visible.

💡

Aim for 4–8 FAQs. Too few look sparse; too many overwhelm visitors. Focus on questions you actually receive from the public, donors, or community members.

13. Managing Staff Accounts & Roles

Adding new team members to the dashboard and controlling their access (Admin only)

🔒

Access required: Only Admin users can create, edit, or delete staff accounts. This section is under Administration → Team & Roles in the sidebar.

Adding a New Staff User

  • Go to Administration → Team & Roles

    You will see a list of all current staff accounts.

  • Click "Add Staff Member"
  • Enter their First Name, Last Name, and Username

    The username is what they will type to log in. Keep it simple — usually their first name or initials in lowercase, e.g. tendai or p.moyo.

  • Enter their Email Address

    This is optional but useful for password reset communications.

  • Set a temporary Password

    Choose something the new staff member can use to log in for the first time. Ask them to change it immediately after their first login.

  • Choose their Role

    Select Admin, Manager, or Writer based on what they need access to. When in doubt, start with Writer and upgrade later if needed.

  • Click "Save"

    The account is created. Share the username and temporary password with the new staff member privately.

Resetting a Staff Member's Password

If a staff member has forgotten their password, an Admin can set a new one for them:


  • Find the staff member in Administration → Team & Roles
  • Click their Edit button

    You can set a new password for them directly from the edit form.

  • Save and share the new password privately

Removing a Staff Member

When a staff member leaves Wild4Life, remove their dashboard access immediately. Click the Delete button next to their name on the Team & Roles list and confirm the deletion. They will no longer be able to log in.

⚠️

Security reminder: Remove dashboard access promptly when a staff member leaves the organisation. Do not allow former staff to retain login credentials.

14. Site Settings

Updating contact details, social media, and legal registration info (Admin only)

🔒

Access required: Only Admin users can access Site Settings. Go to Configuration → Site Settings in the sidebar.

Site Settings is where you update the organisation's official information that appears throughout the public website. Changes here take effect immediately across every page.

Contact & Location

FieldWhat to put here
AddressThe physical address of the Wild4Life office.
EmailThe main contact email address (e.g. info@wild4life.org.zw).
WhatsApp NumberThe WhatsApp number for public enquiries (include country code, e.g. +263).
Phone (Primary)The main landline or mobile number for calls.
Phone (Secondary)A backup phone number (optional).
Office HoursFor example: "Monday–Friday, 08:00–17:00".
Google Maps Embed URLThe embed URL from Google Maps for the office location (shown as an embedded map on the Contact page).

Social Media Links

Enter the full URL for each social media page:

  • Facebook: e.g. https://www.facebook.com/Wild4LifeOrg
  • Instagram: e.g. https://www.instagram.com/wild4lifeorg
  • LinkedIn: e.g. https://www.linkedin.com/company/wild4life

These links power the social media icons that appear in the website footer. Leave a field blank if Wild4Life does not have that social media account.

Certificate & Registration Information

FieldWhat to put here
PVO NumberThe Private Voluntary Organisation registration number (e.g. 40/2017).
Trust Deed NumberThe legal trust deed reference number (e.g. MA909/2012).
Certificate ImageUpload a photo or scan of the registration certificate to display on the website.
Certificate CaptionA short title for the certificate (e.g. "PVO Certificate of Registration").
Certificate DescriptionA one or two sentence explanation of what the certificate means.

Homepage Key Statistics

Three numbers appear prominently on the public homepage to show Wild4Life's impact. Update these periodically to keep them accurate:

  • Years of Experience: How many years Wild4Life has been operating.
  • Districts Covered: How many districts the organisation works in.
  • Overall Score: A performance or satisfaction score (e.g. from an external audit).
⚠️

After making changes, always click "Save Settings" at the bottom of the page. Changes are not saved automatically.

15. Changing Your Password

Keeping your account secure

Every staff member can change their own password at any time. It is good practice to change your password if you have any reason to believe someone else may know it.

  • Click "Change Password" in the sidebar

    It is located near the bottom of the sidebar menu.

  • Enter your Current Password

    This confirms it is really you making the change.

  • Enter your New Password

    Choose something strong — at least 8 characters, mixing letters and numbers is recommended.

  • Confirm the New Password

    Type the same new password again to confirm there are no typos.

  • Click "Change Password"

    Your password is updated immediately. You will need to use the new password the next time you log in.

💡

Good password habits: Do not share your password with anyone — not even other staff members. If someone else needs access, create them their own account. Never write your password on paper near your computer.
